About Prem

Prem is a Management Consultant & Analytics Manager who values constant self-learning and training to challenge himself in providing only the best service to his clients. He has 15+ years experience working with a range of international organizations helping them address a variety of issues that require specialized research and quantitative focus. His clientele includes some of the largest companies/corporations with business interests in Chemicals, Oil & Gas, Automotive, Defense, E-Commerce and Financial Technology sectors. Prem and his team's solutions and recommendations have generated a collective revenue of more than a $1billion for his clients. He has advanced degress in Chemical Engineering and Mathematical Physics and attaches utmost importance to subject matter. He is a dedicated team player with excellent communication and persuasion skills. Prem speaks fluent English, German, Japanese, Hindi and Tamil and capable of having a basic conversation in Mandarin. He is also a self-taught avid coder with strong programming credentials in C/C++, FORTRAN, Python, R, Perl and Haskell.

THE DEVIL IS IN THE QUANTITATIVE DETAILS

Oftentimes businesses are looking for that little nugget that would make them invincible in today's competitive environment. Large-scale data has become that goldmine to look for the elusive nugget. But without sustained direction, subject matter expertise and quantitative finesse, it would be an expensive exercise in failure to find anything of interest that would improve profitability. Consequently, many contemporary businesses struggle in transforming their enthusiastic early adoption of analytics platforms into scalable solutions.

Prem can help you implement comprehensive analytics solutions that overcome this transformation problem. With extensive academic experience in data mining, curation, method analysis, he has developed a range of problem specific data solutions, whose predictive capabilities are built on top of solid mathematical foundation with controls for checks and balances. Prem understands the pitfalls that come with large datasets and attaches a lot of value to avoid making sweeping judgments about their capabilities.
